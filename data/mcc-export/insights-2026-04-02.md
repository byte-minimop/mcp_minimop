## 16. Key Observations for Beacon's Intelligence Model

### 1. Careers vs Corporate Split
- ~60% careers, ~15% corporate, ~25% unknown/mixed from naming alone
- Active portfolio: ~50/50 split (19 careers vs ~10 corporate vs 11 ambiguous)
- Beacon's `campaign_context` classification (careers/corporate) maps well to this real-world split

### 2. Relocation Recruitment Is a Distinct Pattern
Multiple campaigns specifically target multilingual speakers living outside Portugal/Greece to relocate. This is a specific Concentrix campaign intent:
- German speakers → Portugal (primary active pattern)
- French speakers → Portugal
- Dutch speakers → Portugal
- German speakers → Greece (paused but historically active)
This warrants a distinct `objective` or `campaign_context` flag in Beacon: **recruit_relocation**.

### 3. Client-Brand Campaigns Are Real
Campaigns explicitly named after clients (Vodafone, O2, EE, BT, Swisscom) represent a meaningful portion of the historical portfolio. Beacon should not be surprised by briefs that mention specific end-client brands. These typically use:
- Headline: "Work on behalf of [Client]" or "Full time job working with [Client]"
- Location: Specific city where client's contact center is
- URL: jobs.concentrix.com with specific job requisition ID

### 4. Ad Rotation Default
All active campaigns use "Optimize for clicks" — not "Rotate indefinitely". Beacon should default to this when generating.

### 5. Broad Match Is Off by Default
33 of 40 active campaigns have Broad match keywords OFF. Beacon should default to Off unless the brief explicitly requests awareness/reach expansion.

### 6. UTM Tracking Is Standardized
Three template variants are used across the MCC. Beacon should apply the appropriate one per campaign type:
- Search: `{lpurl}?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign={campaignid}&utm_content={adgroupid}&utm_term={keyword}`
- Display: `{lpurl}?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign={campaignid}&utm_content={creative}&utm_term=display`
- PMax: `{lpurl}?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign={campaignid}&utm_content=pmax_{assetgroupid}&utm_term=pmax`

### 7. The Webhelp / Convergys Legacy Is Real
Multiple accounts still carry Webhelp (now Concentrix Romania, Sweden, Latvia, Norway, Estonia) and Convergys (now Concentrix NA, LATAM) branding in URLs. These should trigger Beacon warnings when detected in a brief's landing page URL.

### 8. Performance Max Is Growing
PMax represents 32.5% of active campaigns but only 14.9% of total campaigns — showing clear growth trajectory. The Portugal and Egypt accounts are leading PMax adoption for recruitment. Beacon's PMax support is timely.

### 9. Quality Score Baseline Is Low
Average QS of 4.1 across the historical portfolio is a low baseline. Beacon's copy quality guidance should aim to push new RSAs to Good/Excellent (targeting QS 7+) as a differentiated value-add.

### 10. Disapproval Risk Is Concentrated in Specific Ad Types
The primary disapproval driver is legacy ETAs (deprecated format). Responsive Search Ads have a 482-row disapproval count — worth investigating what policy violations are triggering this. Employment-related policy restrictions in EMEA could be a contributing factor (the EMEA General account has 38% disapproval).

### 11. Phone Extensions Are Rare
Only 3 phone number extensions across 24 accounts. Call extensions are not a standard tactic in this MCC.

### 12. Conversion Tracking Is Minimal in Export
Only 3 conversion action rows in the export — `Calls from ads`, `Do not count calls`. Google Ads Editor exports don't fully capture conversion actions. Beacon should not assume conversion actions are absent just because they're not in this file.

## 17. Recommended Beacon Brain Updates

Based on this analysis, the following improvements are recommended:

### A. Add `recruit_relocation` as a recognized careers sub-objective
When a brief mentions relocation, Beacon should:
- Expect multilingual language targeting (home country language + Portuguese/Greek)
- Recommend radius or country-level targeting for origin countries
- Recognize that landing pages may be `jobs.concentrix.com/global/en/job/[ReqID]` format
- Surface language-specific ad copy recommendations

### B. Validate legacy URL domains
Beacon should flag these legacy landing page domains as Hard gaps:
- `www.convergys.com`
- `WorkatConvergys.com`
- `convergys.wd1.myworkdayjobs.com`
- `www.servicesource.com`
- `offerpop.com`
- `webhelp.acquitysoftware.com`
- `jobs.webhelp.com`

### C. Populate UTM tracking template defaults by campaign type
The three UTM patterns above are battle-tested in this MCC. Beacon should apply them automatically.

### D. Recognize GOO_ and R45_ naming conventions
When a brief comes from accounts using these conventions, Beacon can optionally auto-generate compliant campaign names from the job requisition ID + city + language + date.
